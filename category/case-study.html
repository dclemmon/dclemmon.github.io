2nd Grade Beethoven: History in the Elementary Classroom

How:

Purpose:

The purpose of this case study is to consider what young children can take away from Music lessons that include guided listening in conjunction with historical study.

Benefits and Risks:

The potential risks to participants in this case study are minimal. While the students will not directly benefit from the study beyond the information they will gain in the lesson, the benefit will be in the information gained for the purpose of pedagogical improvement in elementary music classrooms.

Confidentiality:

Names of participants will only be included on consent forms and assent forms and not used in the data collection process or the data analysis process. Additionally, all forms will be kept locked and secure with Dr. Angela Christian, Music History professor at Colorado State University, for three years as per the standard practice and requirements before being destroyed. Should the thesis be published, all confidentiality protocols will be followed.

Contact Information:

Please contact Cassandra Lemmon at cassandra.lemmon@gmail.com with any questions at any point throughout the process. Please also R.S.V.P. to this email address so that enough materials can be made ready for all participants.

Conditions of Participation:

Participants must be in 2nd or 3rd grade (7-9 years old) from any school or district. They must have a consent form signed by a parent or legal guardian as well as an assent form expressing their willingness to participate. Students may choose to leave the study at any time and contact a parent or guardian for pick-up without penalty, at which time their data will be excluded from the study. All forms can be found below and paper copies will be made available at the study site.
